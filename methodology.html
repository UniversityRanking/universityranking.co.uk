🧠 How Our Rankings Work (Methodology)

At UniversityRanking.co.uk, we believe that the best way to choose a university is to hear from students who’ve actually been there and done it. That’s why our rankings are based entirely on what students say about their experience – not on research, reputation, or entry grades.

📋 What is the NSS?

Our rankings are based on something called the National Student Survey (NSS). This is a survey run every year across the UK where students in their final year of undergraduate study are asked to rate their university experience.

Each student is asked the same 28 questions, covering everything from teaching quality to academic support, feedback, mental wellbeing services, and more. For each question, students choose how much they agree or disagree.

We then use this data – not marketing, not league tables, just real answers from real students.

📊 Which answers do we use?

We use the “positivity” score for each question – that means the percentage of students who either agree or strongly agree with the statement. For example, if 80% of students agreed or strongly agreed that their course was intellectually stimulating, the score would be 80%.

We only include answers from all undergraduate students – not just final-year students, so it gives a fuller picture of student experience across the course.

❌ Questions we leave out

The NSS includes 28 questions in total, but we only use 26 in our rankings.

That’s because Question 27 and Question 28 are not asked in all four nations of the UK – only in England. To keep the comparison fair between universities in Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland, and England, we’ve removed these two questions from our calculations.

📆 Smoothing out the data: Using multiple years

To avoid giving too much weight to a single year of results (which might be affected by things like a temporary issue with a course or a new building being renovated), we combine two years of data when calculating scores:

  • For the 2024 rankings, we use 2024 and 2023 data
  • For the 2023 rankings, we use 2023 and 2022 data

Each year is weighted equally (50/50) in our calculations.

If a university or subject doesn’t have data published for both years, we don’t include it – this helps make sure our rankings are based on complete, reliable data.

🧮 How we calculate the scores

Each university gets a score out of 100% for every one of the 26 included questions.

Each question is weighted equally – that means each one contributes 1/26th of the final score.

We then average the 26 scores to create an overall score.

💡 What we include – and what we don’t

✅ We include all UK universities that appear in the official NSS dataset published by the Office for Students (OfS).

❌ We remove small colleges and specialist institutions like drama schools and conservatoires. We’ve done this to match what other league tables do, so it’s easier for you to compare.

🖥 How the scores are shown

  • On the homepage and subject pages, we show scores to two decimal places – for example, 83.17%
  • When you click on a university to view full question-by-question scores, we round each individual score to the nearest whole number – for example, 83%

🛠 Our Customiser Tool

We know not everyone cares about the same things. Maybe mental health support is a priority for you. Maybe you want to focus on teaching quality or assessment feedback.

That’s where our Customiser tool comes in.

You can adjust how important each of the 26 questions is to you personally, using sliders that go from:

  • -50% (makes that question count less – or even reduce a score)
  • 0% (ignore the question entirely)
  • +50% (make that question count more)

We’ll then recalculate the rankings based on your preferences.

📦 Where the data comes from

We only use data published directly by the Office for Students (OfS) – the same public source used by universities, government, and other league tables.

The NSS is updated each year, and so is our site.
